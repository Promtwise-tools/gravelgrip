Buying Guide: What to Look For

  • Width: 38–42mm is ideal for most gravel riding.
  • Tread Pattern: Choose aggressive treads for mud, slicker for hardpack.
  • Tubeless Ready: Reduces punctures, allows lower pressure for better grip.

FAQ

Can I use mountain bike tyres for gravel?
Yes, but MTB tyres are heavier and overkill for most gravel setups.

Are tubeless tyres worth it?
Absolutely. They're more comfortable and puncture-resistant.
